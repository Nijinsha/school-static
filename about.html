FATHER AND FOUNDER OF CONVENTUAL FRANCISCANS

St. FRANCIS OF ASSISSI

St. Francis of Assisi, the father and founder of Conventual Franciscans, was born as one of the seven children of Pietro and his wife Pica, a graet merchant of Assisi, Italy, in 1182 AD. He had lived a luxurious life as a young man, but later through a spiritual enlightment he changed his way of life. He attuned his life according to the teachings of Jesus Christ. Many people were attracted by his teachings and way of life and founded the order of Friars Minors ( the Fransiscans). He died on the evening of October 3rd,1226. On July 16th, he was pronounced a Saint by Pope Gregory IX.

St. Francis was a lover of nature and all the crteatures. On November 29th, 1979, Pope John Paul II declared St. Francis to be the parton of Ecology. As a friend of the poor who was loved by God's creatures, St. Francis invited all of creation; animals, plants, natural forces, even Brother Sun and Sister Moon to give honor and praise to Lord.

Today the followers of St. Francis carry out the same activities of St. Francis all over the world especially loving God and loving His creatures in various ways. Thousands of youngmen follow his footsteps as Franciscans, caring for the poorest of ther poor.

V

History

H
